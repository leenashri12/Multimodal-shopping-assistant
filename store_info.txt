# Product Care and Storage Guidelines

## Honey Storage
Store organic raw honey, clover honey, and wildflower honey at room temperature in a dry place, away from direct sunlight. Ensure the jar is sealed tightly. Pure honey does not spoil or grow bacteria, but it will naturally crystallize over time. If your honey crystallizes, simply place the jar in a bowl of warm water for 15 minutes to liquefy it. Do not refrigerate honey (as it speeds up crystallization) and do not microwave it (as high heat degrades its natural enzymes).

## Cooking Oils (Olive Oil, Flaxseed Oil, Avocado Oil)
Keep cooking oils in a cool, dark place (like a kitchen pantry or cabinet) away from heat sources like stoves, ovens, and direct sunlight. Keep the bottles tightly closed to limit exposure to oxygen, which causes oxidation and rancidity. Note: Flaxseed oil is highly sensitive to light and heat; it must be stored in the refrigerator after opening and consumed within 6 to 8 weeks.

## Tea and Coffee Care
Keep tea leaves, green tea bags, and coffee beans in an airtight, opaque container at room temperature. Moisture, light, and heat are the enemies of freshness. Keep coffee beans whole and grind them right before brewing to lock in the flavor. Never refrigerate or freeze coffee beans or tea leaves, because opening the cold container at room temperature draws condensation, which ruins the delicate flavor oils.

## Nuts, Seeds, and Granola
Store raw almonds, cashews, chia seeds, and granola in airtight containers in a cool, dry place. For extended freshness (especially for bulk nuts and seeds), keep them in the refrigerator or freezer to prevent their natural oils from spoiling. Granola can remain in the pantry for up to 3 months when sealed.

## Dairy Alternatives (Almond, Oat, Coconut, Soy Milk)
Unopened cartons of non-dairy milk (such as almond milk, oat milk, or soy milk) can be safely stored in the pantry at room temperature until the expiration date. Once opened, you must store the carton in the refrigerator, keep it tightly sealed, and consume it within 7 to 10 days. Do not freeze non-dairy milk, as it ruins the emulsion and texture.
